Because the source has been on since t = 0, the field — and hence the force on the mobile particle 1 — depends on the entire time-history of the source, not the instantaneous configuration.  For a pinned point source this PDE solves in closed form, φ(r,t) = (p₁/4πD)·E₁(r²/4Dt), giving a purely central force F(r,t) = p₁/(2πD·r)·exp(-r²/4Dt) directed toward the source, with p₂ the mobile particle's inertia. An agent that fits only instantaneous force-vs-distance sees an inconsistent, apparently noisy law; recovering the true law requires varying the experiment duration and probe distance and recognising the outward-spreading diffusive front and its history dependence.
