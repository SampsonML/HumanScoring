Ten particles interact through a static scalar field obeying the 2D Poisson equation, ∇²φ = source — exactly the same 'gravity' law as the baseline two-particle gravity world. The single piece of hidden structure is inertial mass: the acceleration of each particle is the net 1/r force divided by its mass, and the ten particles fall into three hidden mass groups — particles 0–3 have mass 1, particles 4–6 have mass 2, and particles 7–9 have mass 4 (only the ratios 1 : 2 : 4 are physically identifiable).  All particles experience the identical gravitational field; heavier particles simply respond with proportionally smaller acceleration, tracing visibly flatter trajectories.  An agent that assumes equal masses will fit the light particles tolerably but systematically mispredict the sluggish heavy ones; recovering the law requires recognising the 1/r mutual gravity and the three-way inertial partition from the differing kinematic responses alone.
